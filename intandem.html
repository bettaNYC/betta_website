About the Organization

What is
In&Tandem?

InTandem is a 501(c)(3) non-profit based in New York City with one belief: cycling belongs to everyone. They pair sighted "captains" with visually impaired "stokers" on tandem bikes — giving people who are blind or have low vision the chance to ride free of charge across every borough of the city. No experience necessary. No cost. Just movement.

Visit intandembike.org →
🚲 How it works

A sighted captain steers from the front while the visually impaired stoker pedals from the back. InTandem provides all the bikes and runs thorough training on safety, communication, and tandem technique before every ride goes out.

🗽 Where they ride

Weekly group rides through Central Park and across the five boroughs, plus a full season of event rides — museums, food tours, cultural routes. They also join all major NYC rides: TD Five Boro, NY Century, Tour de Bronx, Tour de Staten Island.

👁 Who they serve

People who are blind, have low vision, or cannot ride independently. For many stokers — especially those who lost their sight later in life — tandem cycling returns something they thought was gone forever: the joy of speed and motion.

🌱 InTandem Youth

A spring program pairing sighted and vision-impaired teens, teaching leadership, teamwork, communication, and cycling skills together. The road is wider when you share it.
